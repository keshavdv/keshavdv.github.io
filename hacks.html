Here are a few past hackathon projects I've worked on.

Webcube

GitHubChallengePost

Webcube is a port of the popular Dolphin Gamecube/Wii emulator, allowing users to play Super Smash Bros over the internet! By writing a networked USB keypad emulator and using Guacamole for display tunneling, we enabled interactive, online multiplayer from anywhere in the world. Built for HackPrinceton Fall 2014.

LinuxBox

GitHubChallengePost

LinuxBox is a web service that allows users to embed a terminal window with complete shell access into their websites, providing full access to a remotely-hosted ephemeral virtual machine. LinuxBox is backed by a Docker infrastructure running on Microsoft Azure. Created for CalHacks 2014.

The Muffin Man

ChallengePost

The Muffin Man is a mashup game, combining Oculus Rift, a Myo armband, and a mobile phone to create a first-person experience like never before. Players can move around the VR environment (using the phone) while shooting muffins at enemies (using their Myo controlled bazooka). Built for Big Red Hacks 2014.Winner of Goldman Sachs Favorite Pick and Best Use of Myo

Impulse

ChallengePostDemo

Impulse is designed to empty the wallets of impulsive shoppers. In essence, Impulse is a flash shopping webite that gives you just 7 seconds to decide to purchase an item. Once the time expires, the item is gone forever! Built for PennApps Spring 2014.
